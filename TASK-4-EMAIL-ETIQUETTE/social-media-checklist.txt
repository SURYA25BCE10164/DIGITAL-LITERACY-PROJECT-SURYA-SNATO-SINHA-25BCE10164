TASK 4(B) : SOCIAL MEDIA DO’S AND DON’Ts

Checklist for Responsible Social Media Use Among College Students
In today’s digital age, social media plays a significant role in communication, networking, and self-expression. However, it is important for college students to use these platforms responsibly to maintain a positive digital presence and avoid potential risks. The following checklist highlights key do’s and don’ts for responsible social media use.
Do’s
Be respectful: Communicate politely and consider others’ opinions before responding.
Verify information: Cross-check facts before sharing to prevent spreading misinformation.
Maintain professionalism: Keep profiles clean and appropriate, especially for academic and career opportunities.
Protect privacy: Adjust privacy settings and avoid sharing sensitive personal information.
Think before posting: Ensure your content reflects positively on you in the long term.
Don’ts
Avoid offensive content: Do not post or share content that is abusive, discriminatory, or harmful.
Do not overshare: Refrain from posting personal details like location, phone number, or daily routines.
Avoid online conflicts: Do not engage in arguments, trolling, or negative discussions.
Respect originality: Do not copy or post others’ content without proper credit.
Limit usage: Avoid excessive use of social media that may affect academic performance and productivity.
